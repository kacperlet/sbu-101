Our Mission

The gender wage gap is a persistent societal issue affecting both men and women across diverse professions. Our mission is to actively address and raise awareness about the disparities in earnings between men and women, with a particular focus on the United States. Extending beyond, we are also dedicated to inspire action, influence policy changes, and contribute to a more just and equitable society for all.

The gender wage gap remains a critical problem in the United States, with women consistently earning less than their men for equal work, posing a significant barrier to achieving workplace equality. This enduring disparity not only hinders economic growth but also emphasizes the urgent need for strategies to address the underlying factors perpetuating unequal compensation. Closing the gap is essential for fostering workplace equity and empowering women in their professional pursuits.

What is the wage gap?

The gender wage gap, reflecting the disparity in incomes between men and women, has evolved over time in the United States, yet persistent challenges remain brought on by several factors on various levels. The contributing factors to the gender wage gap encompasses gender discrimination, occupational segregation, and societal expectations.

These factors have consequences that span the workforce, society, and individual lives. They constrain women's access to higher-paying jobs, lower their earnings, and influence their career decisions. The workforce often sees an increased lack of diversity in leadership roles, contributing to gender-based occupational segregation. Society norms and expectations, influenced by gender inequality, may limit opportunities for women in various stages of life, extending beyond impacting women's personal assets and living conditions.

Gender wage gap is an ongoing problem in our society. As of 2022, for every dollar men made, women only made 78 cents.

Why is that? Well, a large majority of women believe the reason for the wage gap between men and women is purely because of sexism; women being treated differently than men

Other women believe the issue lies in the role that women take in the household; caring for children, keeping the house in order, food shopping, etc. These women are unable to give jobs their full attention since they have a slew of other responsibilities that many men do not have, such as unpaid maternity leave

Regardless of the reason for the wage gap, women working full-time earned just 82% of men's earnings as of 2022.

How Do We Get Involved?

To get involved with this cause, we have conducted research on various organizations and protests dedicated to addressing the gender wage gap. Our plan involves reaching out to these organizations to explore collaboration opportunities, with the goal of jointly developing campaigns to raise awareness. Furthermore, we are considering perceiving past protests related to the gender wage gap, with the option of either supporting existing protests or organizing new protests. The overarching objective is to draw attention to this issue and educate individuals about the unequal pay women are facing for performing equivalent work compared to men.

Organizations to Look into

Equal Pay Today

An American non-profit women's rights organization that was founded in 1974 that advocates for equal pay and equal opportunities for women.

Institute for Women's Policy Research

"A nonpartisan, nonprofit organization that engages in research and dissemination to shape public policy and improve the lives and opportunities of women from diverse backgrounds."

Equal Pay International Coalition

"Led by the ILO, UN Women, and the OECD. The Coalition's goal is to achieve equal pay for women and men everywhere. By bringing together a diverse set of actors with different areas of focus and expertise, EPIC supports governments, employers, workers, and their organizations to make concrete and coordinated progress towards this goal."

American Association of University Women

An American orgranization that has advocated for women since 1881. They fight the constant battle for equal pay and economic opportunities for all women.
